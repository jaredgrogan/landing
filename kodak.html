Kodak Film Explorer

Kodak Double X 5222/7222 (Black & White Negative Stock)

A gritty, high-contrast black-and-white film stock used in iconic films like Raging Bull and Schindler's List.

Year of Release: 1959

Notable Films: Raging Bull, Schindler's List

Best Feature: Deep blacks, rich grain, classic noir look.

Why Choose It? Timeless and gritty, perfect for emotionally charged historical films.

Kodak Tri-X 7266 (Black & White Reversal Stock)

Sharp, punchy black-and-white film stock, known for experimental and documentary filmmaking.

Year of Release: 1954

Notable Films: Man with a Movie Camera, The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari

Best Feature: Sharp, high contrast, documentary feel.

Why Choose It? Perfect for experimental or impactful visual storytelling.

Kodak Vision 3 50D 5203/7203 (Color Negative Stock)

Fine grain, true-to-life colors, ideal for daylight shoots in films like Dunkirk.

Year of Release: 2007

Notable Films: Dunkirk, Once Upon a Time in Hollywood

Best Feature: Stunning detail and accurate color reproduction.

Why Choose It? Perfect for bright outdoor scenes with natural colors.
